# Dear Customer Letter Template

## Instructions

The Dear Customer Letter is a Working Backwards format used for incremental improvements to existing products — features, enhancements, or changes that don't warrant a full PR/FAQ but still need customer-centric articulation.

**When to use a Dear Customer Letter instead of a PR/FAQ:**
- Feature addition to an existing product (not a new product)
- Improvement that affects existing customers (not new market entry)
- Change that needs customer communication (deprecation, migration, pricing change)
- Enhancement where the customer context is already well-understood
- Initiative that takes 2-8 weeks (not months)

**When to upgrade to a full PR/FAQ:**
- If the letter exceeds 3 pages, the initiative is bigger than you think — write a PR/FAQ
- If you're creating a fundamentally new experience (not improving an existing one)
- If leadership needs to make a resource allocation decision beyond your team's budget

**Format:** Written as an actual letter from the product team to customers. Conversational but precise. 2-3 pages maximum.

---

## DEAR CUSTOMER LETTER

**Product/Feature:** [Name]

**Author:** [Name]

**Date:** [Date]

**Customer segment:** [Who is receiving this letter]

---

Dear [Customer Segment Name],

### Opening: What's Changing

We're writing to tell you about [specific change/improvement]. Starting [date or timeframe], you'll be able to [new capability or improvement] when you [trigger/context for when they'd use it].

*[Tip: Be specific about what's different. "We're improving performance" is vague. "Your dashboard will load in under 2 seconds, down from the current 8-12 seconds" is specific.]*

### Why We Built This

We heard from many of you that [specific customer feedback or pain point]. In our research, we found that [data point supporting the need]:

- [Evidence point 1: e.g., "67% of you reported that X takes too long"]
- [Evidence point 2: e.g., "Support tickets about Y increased 40% last quarter"]
- [Evidence point 3: e.g., "In interviews, you told us Z was your top frustration"]

We took this seriously because [connection to customer impact — time, money, frustration, risk].

*[Tip: Show customers you listened. Reference their actual feedback, not your internal roadmap priorities.]*

### What You'll Experience

Here's what changes for you:

**Before (today):**
[Describe the current experience in 3-4 concrete steps. Be honest about the pain points.]

1. [Current step 1 — include time/effort/frustration]
2. [Current step 2]
3. [Current step 3]
4. [Current result — including what's unsatisfying about it]

**After (with this change):**
[Describe the new experience in 3-4 concrete steps. Show the improvement at each step.]

1. [New step 1 — highlight what's different and better]
2. [New step 2]
3. [New step 3]
4. [New result — including the measurable improvement]

**The key improvement:** [Single sentence: what metric improves by how much. Example: "What used to take 45 minutes now takes 3 minutes."]

*[Tip: The before/after comparison should make the value immediately obvious. If a reader can't see clear improvement in the side-by-side, the feature isn't valuable enough or you haven't articulated it well.]*

### What You Need to Do

[Choose one of these based on the change type:]

**Option A — No action required:**
This improvement will be available automatically on [date]. You don't need to change anything about how you use [product]. You'll simply notice that [specific observable improvement].

**Option B — Opt-in:**
This feature is available starting [date]. To enable it:
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

We recommend trying it with [low-risk starting point] first.

**Option C — Migration required:**
This change requires a one-time migration. Here's what you need to know:
- **Timeline:** Migration must be completed by [date]
- **Effort:** Approximately [time estimate] for a typical setup
- **Support:** We've prepared [migration guide / automated tool / support channel]
- **What happens if you don't migrate:** [Honest description of consequences]

*[Tip: Be clear about customer effort required. Underestimating migration effort destroys trust faster than any feature builds it.]*

### What This Doesn't Change

We want to be transparent about what stays the same:

- [Thing customers might worry about changing — "Your existing configurations remain untouched"]
- [Limitation that persists — "This doesn't yet support [edge case], which we plan to address in [timeframe]"]
- [Pricing impact — "No pricing changes associated with this improvement" OR "This is included in your current plan" OR explicit pricing change if applicable]

*[Tip: Proactively address fears. Customers who've been burned by "improvements" that break their workflow need explicit reassurance about what's preserved.]*

### What's Coming Next

Based on your continued feedback, we're also working on:

1. **[Next improvement, near-term]** — [One sentence, expected timeframe]
2. **[Future improvement, medium-term]** — [One sentence, expected timeframe]

We'll share more details on these as they get closer.

*[Tip: This shows customers you're invested in the problem space, not just shipping one fix and moving on. Keep it to 2-3 items max. Don't over-promise.]*

### Your Feedback

We'd love to hear from you:
- [Feedback channel: email, survey link, community forum, in-product feedback button]
- [Specific question you'd like answered: "Is this solving the right problem?" or "What should we prioritize next?"]

Thank you for being a [product] customer. We're committed to [brief mission statement related to this improvement area].

[Sign-off]
[Team/Product Name] Team

## INTERNAL REVIEW CHECKLIST

Before sending to customers or submitting for review:

- [ ] Letter is ≤3 pages (if longer, consider upgrading to PR/FAQ)
- [ ] Opening clearly states what's changing in one sentence
- [ ] "Why We Built This" cites actual customer data (not internal reasoning)
- [ ] Before/After comparison makes value immediately obvious
- [ ] Customer action required is explicit and accurate
- [ ] Limitations are disclosed honestly (not hidden)
- [ ] No jargon or technical terms the customer wouldn't use
- [ ] A customer could forward this to a colleague and they'd understand it
- [ ] Timeline and dates are specific (not "soon" or "in a future release")
- [ ] Reviewed by at least one person outside the team

## DECISION CRITERIA: Letter vs PR/FAQ vs 5CQ

| Signal | Format |
|--------|--------|
| Improving something customers already use | Dear Customer Letter |
| Launching something customers have never seen | PR/FAQ |
| Exploring whether an idea has merit | 5CQ |
| Deprecating or removing a feature | Dear Customer Letter (focus on migration path) |
| Changing pricing | Dear Customer Letter (with extra care on "What's Changing") |
| Entering a new market segment | PR/FAQ |
| Adding a small feature (<1 week of work) | None needed (just good release notes) |
| Strategic initiative with resource allocation decision | PR/FAQ |
